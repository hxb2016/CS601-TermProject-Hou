Tang Dynasty (618-907) was a unified Central Plains dynasty after Sui Dynasty in Chinese history. It had 21 emperors and lasted 289 years. At the end of the Sui Dynasty, Li Yuan, the duke of the Tang Dynasty, took advantage of the situation and raised an army in Jinyang. In 618, he proclaimed himself emperor and established the Tang Dynasty with Chang 'an as its capital. After Emperor Taizong of Tang Dynasty succeeded to the throne, he established the rule of Zhenguan, which laid the foundation for the prosperous Tang Dynasty. Tang Gaozong Chengzhen Guan legacy, created the "rule of permanent emblem". In 690, Wu Zetian changed the title to Zhou, and after the Shenlong Revolution in 705, the title of Tang was restored. During the reign of Emperor Xuanzong of Tang Dynasty, Kaiyuan Flourishing appeared, which made the Tang Dynasty reach its heyday. At the end of Tianbao's reign, the population of China reached about 80 million. After the Rebellion of An and Shi, vassal towns were divided and eunuchs monopolized power, and the national power gradually declined. After the reign of Emperor Xianzong Yuan He Zhongxing, Emperor Wuchang Huichang Zhongxing and Emperor Xuanzong Dazhong, the national power was restored. The ensuing peasant uprising at the end of Tang Dynasty destroyed the foundation of Tang rule and increased the power of Fanzhen. In 907, Zhu Wen, the general of Fanzhen town, usurped the throne and the Tang Dynasty fell. The Tang Dynasty covered an unprecedented territory, reaching its peak from the Sea of Japan in the east, Annam in the south, the Aral Sea in the west, and Lake Baikal in the north. The Tang Dynasty adopted the policy of jimi for the surrounding ethnic groups, and established close political and economic ties with all ethnic groups through conferring, marriage and other ways. Japan, Nanzhao, Silla, Bohai and other countries sent people to learn the system and culture of the Tang Dynasty. As one of the most powerful countries in the world at that time, the Tang Dynasty enjoyed a wide reputation and had many contacts with Asian and European countries. After the Tang Dynasty, overseas Chinese were often called "Tang people". The Tang Dynasty actively exchanged and learned from other countries, which made its economy, society, culture and art show diversified and open features. A large number of famous artists emerged in the fields of poetry, calligraphy, painting and music, such as Li Bai, Du Fu, the master of poetry, Bai Juyi, Yan Zhenqing, the master of painting Wu Daozi, and Li Guian, the musician.

The Song Dynasty (960-1279) was a dynasty in Chinese history that followed five dynasties and ten kingdoms and began with the Yuan Dynasty. It was divided into two phases, the Northern Song and the Southern Song. It had a total of 18 emperors and lasted 319 years. In 960, Zhao Kuangyin, a general of the Forbidden Army of the Later Zhou Dynasty, seized the throne through the Chenqiao mutiny, made Kaifeng Prefecture, Tokyo as the capital, and established the Song Dynasty. Zhao Kuangyin, in order to avoid the chaos of vassal power and eunuch dictatorship in the late Tang Dynasty, adopted the policy of worshiping literature and suppressing military power, strengthened the centralization of power, and deprived the generals of military power. After Emperor Taizong ascended the throne, he put an end to the secession. In 1004, the Song and Liao reached an alliance between Chanyuan and the Song Dynasty was gradually ruled. After Renzong of Song Dynasty, the social crisis of Song Dynasty became more and more serious. After Emperor Shenzong of Song Dynasty ascended the throne, he appointed Wang Anshi and others to carry out reform activities, which achieved some results, but made the Song Dynasty deeply entangled in disputes between the old and new parties. From 1125, the Jin Dynasty invaded the south in a large scale until the capture of Tokyo in 1127 and the collapse of the Northern Song Dynasty. In the same year, Zhao Gou, King of Kang, ascended the throne in Nanjing in the name of Tianfu and established the Southern Song Dynasty. After the signing of the Peace Treaty of Shaoxing, the Southern Song Dynasty was confined to the Qinling Mountains and Huaihe River. During the reign of Emperor Xiaozong and Emperor Ningzong, two expeditions to the north ended in failure. In 1234, the Song allied with the Mongols to destroy the Jin, and fought the Song and Yuan war with the Mongols. In 1276, the Yuan army captured Lin 'an Prefecture. After the Battle of Yashan in 1279, the Southern Song Dynasty fell. The Song Dynasty was a time of high prosperity of commodity economy, culture, education and scientific innovation in Chinese history. Some scholars estimated that in 1000, China's GDP accounted for 22.7% of the world economy at that time. In addition, the Song Dynasty saw the revival of Confucianism and the formation of Neo-Confucianism, the rapid development of science and technology, and relatively enlightened politics. During this period, the population of the Song Dynasty increased rapidly. Some scholars estimated that the total population of the Song Dynasty reached 126 million in 1124. Chinese historian Chen Yinke once said, "The culture of the Chinese nation has evolved over thousands of years, and it originated in the Zhao and Song dynasties." Some scholars in western and Japanese historians also believe that the Song Dynasty was the period of Renaissance and economic revolution in Chinese history.

The Yuan Dynasty (1271-1368) is the first unified dynasty founded by the ethnic minorities in the history of China. The rulers of the Mongoliums are the children of Zhijin. The succession of five dynasties and eleven emperors lasted for 162 years from the establishment of Mongol regime by Genghis Khan in 1206, and 98 years from the establishment of the state title Yuan by Kubilie. After the Yuan Dynasty withdrew from the Central Plains, the Northern Yuan regime lasted until 1402. In 1206, Genghis Khan unified the Mongolian ministries and established the Great Mongolia. The Western Liao, Western Xia, Khwarazm, Jin and other regimes were conquered successively. After the death of Mongkor Khan, a dispute arose between Ariq and Kublai Khan for the throne, prompting the division of Greater Mongolia. In 1260, Kublai Khan ascended the throne as emperor, established the Yuan Dynasty as the "Middle Unification" and made Kaiping Fu (the upper capital) the capital. In 1271, Kublai Khan took the meaning of "Great Zai Qianyuan" in the Book of Changes and changed the title of his kingdom to "Great Yuan" and made Dadu his capital in the following year. In 1279, the exile regime of the Southern Song Dynasty was completely destroyed, ending the long period of division since the end of the Tang Dynasty. After that, the Yuan Dynasty continued its foreign expansion, attacking Japan, Burma, Vietnam, Java, etc., but all failed. In the middle of the Yuan Dynasty, the succession of the imperial throne was disordered and the coup was frequent. The politics was not on the right track, and the Sinicization was delayed and the development was stagnant. In the later period of the Yuan Dynasty, the political corruption, the interference of the powerful ministers, the national and class contradictions were increasingly intensified, leading to the peasant uprising at the end of the Yuan Dynasty. In 1368, Zhu Yuanzhang founded the Ming Dynasty, and then launched a northern expedition to expel the Yuan Court and capture Dadu. Since then, the Yuan Court retreated to Mobei, known as the Northern Yuan. In 1402, Yuan minister Lili usurped the power to establish Tatar, and the Northern Yuan collapsed. During the Yuan Dynasty, the unified multi-ethnic state was further consolidated and its territory exceeded that of previous dynasties. The Yuan Dynasty abolished Shangshu Province and Menxia Province, and retained Zhongshu province with the Privy Council and the Yushitai in charge of government, military and supervision. Local provinces implemented the provincial system, which was the first of its kind in China. The Yuan Dynasty was prosperous in commodity economy and overseas trade, and had frequent diplomatic exchanges with other countries. In terms of culture, Yuan Opera and other cultural forms appeared, which became more secular.

The Ming Dynasty (1368 -- 1644), a dynasty in Chinese history, was founded by Zhu Yuanzhang, Emperor Taizu of the Ming Dynasty. The capital was established in Nanjing in the early period and moved to Beijing in the Ming Chengzu period. Sixteen emperors, 276 years in total. At the end of the Yuan, the Red Scarf uprising broke out, and Zhu Yuanzhang joined Guo Zixing's team. In 1364, he became King of Wu, known as Western Wu in history. In early 1368, he proclaimed himself emperor, with the title of Daming and the capital of Nanjing. In 1421, Zhu Di moved the capital to Beijing and made Nanjing his secondary capital. After the reign of Hongwu, the reign of Yongle, the reign of Renxuan and so on, the early Ming Dynasty had clear politics and strong national strength. In 1449, by the change of civil castle from prosperity to decline, after Hongzhi Zhongxing, Wanli Zhongxing national power recovery. In the late Ming Dynasty, due to political corruption, Donglin party rivalry and natural disasters, the national strength declined and peasant uprising broke out. In 1644, Li Zicheng invaded Beijing, Emperor Chongzhen hanged himself, the Ming Dynasty collapsed, and the Qing army entered the customs. The imperial family of the Ming Dynasty established a number of regimes in the south, known as the Southern Ming Dynasty. The Qing army successively defeated the regimes of Hongguang, Longwu and Shaowu. In 1662, Emperor Yongli was killed and the Southern Ming Dynasty was destroyed. In 1683, the Qing army captured Taiwan, and Ming Zheng of Zhengshuo of Fengming Dynasty was overthrown. The territory of the Ming Dynasty included the Han Dynasty, reaching the Sea of Japan and the outer Hinggan Mountains in the northeast, and then shrinking to the Liaohe River basin. North Da Yin Mountain, retreat to the Ming Great Wall; In the west, it reached Hami, Xinjiang, and then retreated to Jiayuguan. Southwest to Burma and Siam in the north, and then back to about the present Yunnan; And in the Tibetan region has the Jimi garrison, also had recovered Annan. During the Ming Dynasty, the absolute monarchy was strengthened unprecedentedly, and the multi-ethnic countries were further unified and consolidated. In the early Ming Dynasty, the prime minister was dismissed and the factory guard spy agency was set up, which strengthened the centralization of authoritarianism, but also foreshadowed the eunuch dictatorship in the middle and late period. The anti - feudalism struggle of peasants in Ming Dynasty also entered a new stage. During the Ming Dynasty, handicraft industry and commodity economy prospered, a large amount of commercial capital was transformed into industrial capital, and commercial market towns and the budding of capitalism appeared. Culture and art show the trend of secularization. According to the Ming Shi Record, the peak population was 71.85 million, but there are different accounts of over 100 million and nearly 200 million

The Qing Dynasty (1636-1912), the last feudal dynasty in Chinese history, had a total of 12 emperors ruled by Aisin Gioro. Since Nurhachi founded the later Jin, total 296 years. Since Emperor Huangtaiji changed the title of the kingdom to Qing Dynasty, it lasted 276 years. It was 268 years from the beginning of the army clearance and the establishment of a national regime. In 1616, Nurhachi, the Nuzhen leader of Jianzhou, established the Later Jin Dynasty. In 1636, the three ethnic groups of Han, Manchu and Mongolian submitted a proposal of persuasion. Huangtaiji proclaimed himself emperor and changed the kingdom's title to Qing Dynasty. In 1644, Wu Sangui, the Ming general stationed at Shanhaiguan, surrendered to the Qing Dynasty, and Duergun led the Qing troops to pass. Within 20 years after entering the customs, the regimes of Dashun, Daxi and Nanming were pacified. After that, he put down the chaos of San Francisco, unified Taiwan, and completed the national reunification. During the three dynasties of Kangyongqian, China's traditional society achieved unprecedented development achievements. Land reclamation, abundant products, the social life of the small-scale peasant economy is prosperous and stable, and the overall national strength is strong. After the Opium War, many of them were invaded by foreign powers and carried out modernization exploration and reform such as the Westernization Movement and the Reform Movement of 1898. On February 12, 1912, Beiyang Minister Yuan Shikai induced the Qing Emperor Puyi to abdicate and issued an edict on the abdication of the Qing Emperor, which ended the Qing Dynasty. During the Qing Dynasty, the unified multi-ethnic state was consolidated and developed. The rulers of the Qing Dynasty unified the Mongolian provinces, incorporated Xinjiang and Tibet into the territory, and actively safeguarded the integrity of national territorial sovereignty. During the reign of Qianlong, China was finally established as a unified multi-ethnic world power. At its peak, the Qing Dynasty reached as far as the Scallion Ridge and Balkhash Lake in the west, the Tangnuliang Sea in the northwest, Mobei and Siberia in the north, the Pacific Ocean (including Sakhalin Island) in the east, and the Nansha Islands in the south. Including more than 50 ethnic groups, the country is unprecedentedly unified. During this period, the absolutism of ancient China reached its peak. [21] In the early Qing Dynasty, agriculture and commerce were developed, and dense commercial cities appeared in the south of the Yangtze River, and large commercial clans appeared in the whole country. On this basis, the population passed the 400 million mark, accounting for nearly half of the world's total population of one billion.

The People's Republic of China (hereinafter referred to as "China"), founded on October 1, 1949, is a socialist country under the people's democratic dictatorship led by the working class and based on the alliance of workers and peasants, located in the east of Asia and on the west coast of the Pacific Ocean. With the five-star red flag as the national flag, the "March of the Volunteers" as the national anthem, and the national emblem as the national flag, Tian 'anmen, gear and ears of wheat and rice, the common language is Mandarin and standard Chinese characters. The capital of Beijing is a unified multi-ethnic country composed of 56 ethnic groups with the Han as the main body. China has a land area of about 9.6 million square kilometers, a coastline of more than 18,000 kilometers in the east and south, and a total sea area of about 4.73 million square kilometers. There are more than 7,600 islands, of which Taiwan Island is the largest with an area of 35,798 square kilometers. China has borders with 14 countries and maritime neighbors with eight countries. The provincial-level administrative regions are divided into 23 provinces, 5 autonomous regions, 4 municipalities directly under the Central Government and 2 special administrative regions. China is one of the countries with the longest history in the world, with a splendid culture and glorious revolutionary tradition. China leads the world in the number of world heritage sites. After the founding of New China in 1949, it entered a period of socialist revolution and construction. In 1956, the transition to socialism was realized. Since then, socialist construction has made twists and turns in its exploration. After the end of the "Cultural Revolution", we carried out reform and opening up and concentrated our efforts on socialist modernization along the road of socialism with Chinese characteristics. After years of hard work, socialism with Chinese characteristics has entered a new era. China is the developing country with the largest population, the third largest land area and the second largest economy in the world. It will continue to be the largest contributor to global economic growth, with its economic aggregate exceeding 100 trillion yuan by 2020. China adheres to an independent foreign policy of peace. As a permanent member of the United Nations Security Council and an important member of many international organizations, China is considered one of the potential superpowers.

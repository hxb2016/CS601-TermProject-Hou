Introduction

Nanyang, known as Wan in ancient times, is a prefecture-level city and provincial sub-central city in Henan Province. It is an important transportation hub in central China approved by The State Council and a regional central city in the border area of Henan, Hubei and Shaanxi. By the end of 2019, the city had jurisdiction over 2 administrative regions, 10 counties and 1 county-level city; The total area is 26,509 square kilometers. According to the seventh census data, as of midnight on November 1, 2020, the permanent population of Nanyang city is 9,713,112 people.

Nanyang is a national historical and cultural city with a history of more than 2700 years. It is located in the southwest of Henan Province and the border area of Henan, Hubei and Shaanxi provinces. It is named for its location in the south of Funiu Mountain and the north of the Han River. Nanyang Xichuan is the location of the first hub of Taocha Canal, the middle route of the world's largest water diversion project, and one of the important core water source areas.

Nanyang has a rich history and culture. It is an important birthplace of Chu and Han culture. Allusion or legends such as three visits to the thatched cottage, sheep and fish hanging, Pangu myth, cowherd and Weaver Girl all originate here. It is "the hometown of Chinese rose", "the hometown of world Ai" and "the hometown of Chinese jade carving".
